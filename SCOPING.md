# Starter Project — Predicting the Regulatory Effect of a Noncoding Variant from Sequence

Scoping document. Test case: **rs6733839** at the BIN1 Alzheimer's locus. Verified against Ensembl, JASPAR, and ENCODE APIs on the date of writing (generated by Claude Science).

---

## Part 1 — Methods & tools: the lineage, with citations

The field is a progression from *classify the variant* → *predict the base-resolution signal* → *read the mechanism off it*. Four generations matter for this project.

### 1.1 The DeepSEA / Basset lineage (2015) — sequence → chromatin state
- **DeepSEA** (Zhou & Troyanskaya, *Nature Methods* 2015). First CNN to predict chromatin features (DNase, TF binding, histone marks) from ~1 kb of sequence, then score a variant as the difference in predicted probability between ref and alt allele. This **"ref vs alt delta"** is the template every later method still uses. DOI: 10.1038/nmeth.3547
- **Basset** (Kelley, Snoek & Rinn, *Genome Research* 2016). Same idea, focused on DNase/ATAC accessibility across ~160 cell types; introduced the CNN-for-accessibility architecture ChromBPNet descends from. DOI: 10.1101/gr.200535.115
- **Limitation:** predictions are per-peak, coarse (200 bp–1 kb bins) — they tell you *whether* accessibility changes but not *which* motif or *where*.

### 1.2 Enformer / long-range attention (2021) — sequence → many tracks, long context
- **Enformer** (Avsec et al., *Nature Methods* 2021). Transformer with ~200 kb receptive field, thousands of tracks at 128 bp resolution; better at distal enhancer→promoter links. DOI: 10.1038/s41592-021-01252-x
- **Borzoi** (Linder et al., 2023) extends this to RNA-seq coverage.
- **Use when** the variant may act at a distance. For a variant inside the element it disrupts (our case), base-resolution accessibility models are sharper.

### 1.3 ChromBPNet (2024) — base-resolution accessibility + bias factorization
**ChromBPNet** (Pampari, Shcherbina et al., Kundaje lab). Bias-factorized, base-resolution CNN of ATAC/DNase accessibility. Two properties make it the recommended core:
1. Explicitly models & removes **Tn5 sequence bias**, so learned motifs are biological, not assay artefacts.
2. Resolves TF footprints and supports **contribution scores (DeepSHAP) + in-silico mutagenesis (ISM)** — exactly the "which motif does the variant break" question.

bioRxiv 10.1101/2024.12.25.630221 · PMID 39829783 (PMC11741299) · code: github.com/kundajelab/chrombpnet. Companion tools (bpnet, tfmodisco-lite, modisco) map contributions to known motifs.

### 1.4 The Corces-lab brain models — CNNs on brain ATAC-seq
- **Corces et al., *Nature Genetics* 2020** — "Single-cell epigenomic analyses implicate candidate causal variants at inherited risk loci for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's diseases." scATAC of human brain → cell-type-resolved accessibility atlas + candidate-causal-variant framework. DOI: 10.1038/s41588-020-00721-x (PMC7606627).
- **Regression CNN on brain/immune accessibility** (Corces-associated, *PLOS Comp Biol* 2024, 10.1371/journal.pcbi.1012356) — sequence→accessibility CNN for prioritising regulatory variants.
- **Corces Lab Resources** — corceslab.com/pages/Resources/ — canonical place pretrained brain models & processed tracks are posted (access caveat in Part 2).

**Recommendation:** use **ChromBPNet** as the workhorse (best-documented, maintained, purpose-built for ISM + motif attribution); treat Corces brain models as a **domain-specific cross-check** once the pipeline runs end-to-end.

## Part 2 — What's actually accessible right now

### 2.1 Corces pretrained brain models — reachable but not one-click
Official distribution is the Corces Resources page, but weights come via linked repos / supplementary hosting, formats vary. **Action:** manual-download cross-check, not the primary dependency. Start with ChromBPNet.

### 2.2 ChromBPNet — fully accessible
- Install: `pip install chrombpnet` (or the conda recipe in the repo).
- Pretrained models + Tn5 bias models via the Kundaje lab (github.com/kundajelab/chrombpnet documents the model zoo + ENCODE bias models).
- Can also train your own on the ENCODE dataset below (repo tutorial covers this).

### 2.3 A concrete, verified ENCODE ATAC-seq dataset
Queried ENCODE live. 36 released human/mouse ATAC-seq experiments match "brain." Two human experiments are directly usable, fully processed on GRCh38:

| Accession | Biosample | Processed outputs (GRCh38) |
|---|---|---|
| **ENCSR729FNL** | Posterior cingulate cortex, adult male | fold-change bigWig (ENCFF752ZXK), IDR peaks (ENCFF221FSW), alignments (ENCFF876ELG) |
| **ENCSR802GEV** | Cerebellum, adult male | fold-change bigWig (ENCFF847DVN), IDR peaks (ENCFF858CCP), alignments (ENCFF111JBV) |

Both ship fold-change bigWigs (ChromBPNet input), IDR peaks (training regions), GRCh38 BAMs. **Caveat:** bulk cortical tissue. Our variant acts in **microglia**, so bulk cortex dilutes the microglial signal. Ideal training data = a **microglia / myeloid** accessibility track (e.g. Corces or **Nott 2019** microglial ATAC). Bulk cortex is the accessible fallback to get the pipeline working.

## Part 3 — Test-case variant and the reproducible pipeline

### 3.1 The variant: rs6733839 (BIN1 locus) — verified against Ensembl
- **Location (GRCh38):** chr2:127,135,234 (single-base SNV).
- **Alleles:** C/T (ref C; T = AD-risk / minor allele, MAF ≈ 0.40). Ensembl records C/G/T; disease-relevant contrast is **C→T**.
- **Why:** one of the strongest common AD GWAS signals, ~28 kb upstream of **BIN1** (2nd-strongest AD risk gene after APOE). Fine-mapping + brain cell-type enhancer maps place it in a **microglia-specific enhancer**; the risk allele is proposed to alter a **MEF2** TF binding site. Known, literature-backed hypothesis to validate against.
- **Expected model behavior:** risk (T) allele *reduces* predicted microglial accessibility; ISM localizes the effect to a **MEF2 motif**.

### 3.2 The JASPAR target motif — verified against JASPAR API
- **MEF2A — MA0052.4** (CORE, vertebrates, MADS-box) — primary match.
- **MEF2C — MA0497.1** (CORE, vertebrates, MADS-box) — microglia-relevant paralog, cross-check. Consensus core: `CTAWAAATAG` (MEF2 A/T-rich box).
- **Success** = ISM-disrupted bases at rs6733839 align to this motif.

### 3.3 The reproducible pipeline

```
                 rs6733839  (chr2:127,135,234  C→T, GRCh38)
                        │
        ┌───────────────┴───────────────┐
        ▼                               ▼
  build REF window                build ALT window
  (2,114 bp centered on          (same window, C→T at
   the variant)                    center position)
        │                               │
        └───────────────┬───────────────┘
                        ▼
        ChromBPNet forward pass (ref & alt)
   → predicted base-resolution ATAC profile + total counts
                        │
                        ▼
   Δ = log(alt counts) − log(ref counts)      ← variant effect score
   (expect NEGATIVE: risk allele lowers accessibility)
                        │
                        ▼
   In-silico mutagenesis / DeepSHAP contribution scores
   over the ±30 bp window around the variant
                        │
                        ▼
   Extract the high-contribution subsequence
                        │
                        ▼
   Match to JASPAR with TFMoDISco-lite / FIMO / Tomtom
   → expect hit to MEF2A MA0052.4 (MEF2C MA0497.1)
                        │
                        ▼
   Confirm the C→T base sits inside the MEF2 motif and
   the risk allele weakens the match  → mechanism reproduced
```

**Step-by-step:**
1. **Environment** — conda env: chrombpnet, pyfaidx, pyBigWig, pysam, tfmodisco-lite, modiscolite, pyjaspar/Biopython. GPU for training; scoring one variant runs on CPU.
2. **Reference genome** — download GRCh38 FASTA (Ensembl/UCSC hg38), index with pyfaidx.
3. **Model** — (a) download a pretrained ChromBPNet ATAC + Tn5 bias model from the Kundaje zoo, or (b) train on ENCSR802GEV/ENCSR729FNL. Prefer a microglia/myeloid model if obtainable.
4. **Variant windows** — extract the input-length window (2,114 bp for ATAC ChromBPNet) centered on chr2:127,135,234; make ref (C) + alt (T) copies.
5. **Predict** — run both; record profile + summed counts. Effect = log-fold-change of counts (+ Jensen–Shannon distance of profiles).
6. **ISM / attribution** — DeepSHAP contributions across the window + saturation ISM over ±30 bp; variant should land on a high-contribution base.
7. **Motif match** — TFMoDISco-lite on contributions (or FIMO/Tomtom) vs JASPAR CORE vertebrates; confirm MEF2 (MA0052.4 / MA0497.1) overlaps the variant.
8. **Validate against biology** — sign (T lowers accessibility) + MEF2 identity match the published microglial-enhancer hypothesis.
9. **Reproducibility** — pin versions, fix seeds, log every accession + exact GRCh38 coordinate; save ref/alt windows, deltas, contribution tracks, motif match as artifacts.

### 3.4 Known caveats
- **Cell type matters most.** rs6733839 acts in microglia; a bulk-cortex model may show a muted/absent effect. **The single biggest determinant of success** — chase a microglial/myeloid track.
- **Coordinate/allele hygiene.** Confirm build (GRCh38) and strand before mutating; MEF2 motifs are near-palindromic but not exact — orientation matters for the JASPAR match.
- **ISM ≠ ground truth.** Attribution localizes the *model's* learned signal; corroborate with published fine-mapping + an orthogonal motif scan (FIMO), not ISM alone.
- **Corces model access is manual** — don't block the pipeline on it; ChromBPNet gets you end-to-end first.
